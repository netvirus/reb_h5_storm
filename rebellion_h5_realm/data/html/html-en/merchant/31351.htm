Wharf Manager Volker:
Ah, lately the old memories seem to be floodingback. Long ago, you know, this wharf was a beehive of activity! Tradeships of the Kresnik dropped anchor every day, filled to the gunwaleswith all sorts of wonderful items. Giran to the south -- even Heine --were nothing by comparison. But then the war came, and diplomaticrelations with the Kresnik were cut off. No longer did the wealth-ladentrade ships visit. Today, all we see are small passenger ships. Oh, oncein a long while, a foreign vessel carrying a party of adventurers maystop in, but. Sigh. Oh, before I forget: I have heard news that aferry has begun service to Gludin Harbor, which is in the Gludio regionof Aden Kingdom. The delays of the initial trial runs are over, and itis now in full operation.
