When the gods laid the foundations of this world,they appointed the Giants -- strongest of all their creations -- to ruleit as their stewards. Gradually, though, the Giants became disillusionedwith their masters, until they could only see the gods' flaws and noneof their virtues. The seeds of rebellion were born. Rationalizing thatthey had need to defend the themselves, the Giants created both theKamael, including the three Spicula heroes, and mighty weapons capableof inflicting harm on the gods themselves. The Giants grew exceedinglypowerful, and Einhasad became suspicious. She discovered their secreexperiments and became wrathful. Unable to convince the other gods ofthe threat, she in her fury attacked the Giants alone. Taken bysurprise, the Giants were thrown back. Finally, they committed theKamael to the war, and the tide began to turn. The Spicula Harkilgamedused the legendary sword Enuma Elish to slay Einhasad's second daughter,while Katenar bombarded the goddess' army with the mighty cannonLeopold. The battle seemed to shift in favor of the Giants, who rejoicedat the might of their arms. Too soon, as it happened.
